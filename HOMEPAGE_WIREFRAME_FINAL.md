# Final Homepage Wireframe & UX Architecture: Solve

## Global Layout & Fold Strategy
**Layout Principles:** 
- Left-aligned content for a highly readable, manual-like structure.
- High-contrast, tactile UI (thick borders, bold typography, deep shadows).
- Modular sections that flow logically: Problem → Math (Calculate) → Fix (Hardware).

**Above the Fold:** 
- The Header and Hero section must be entirely visible. 
- On desktop, the top of the "Fix Section" (Problem Picker) should peek above the bottom edge of the screen, signaling immediate interactivity without scrolling. 

---

## 1. Header / Navigation
**Purpose:** Quick routing based on user intent (problem-first, math-first, or hardware-first).
- **Logo:** Solve.RuralOpsTools
- **Links (Left-aligned or centered):** Fix | Calculators | Kits 
- **Secondary (Right-aligned):** Favorites (or "My Shed")

---

## 2. Hero Section
**Purpose:** Establish immediate credibility, state the value proposition clearly, and offer immediate pathways to action.
- **Eyebrow:** RURAL DIAGNOSTICS & FIELD TOOLS
- **Headline:** Stop guessing. Find the leak. Fix it fast.
- **Subheadline:** Simple, rugged tools and snap-on monitors to track water leaks, fence faults, and freeze risks without confusing manuals or IT guys.
- **Primary CTA:** Pick Your Problem *(anchors to Fix Section)*
- **Secondary CTA:** See Hardware Kits *(anchors to Kits Section)*
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** Left-aligned on both. On mobile, stack CTAs vertically, full-width.

---

## 3. Fix Section (Problem Picker)
**Purpose:** Route the user directly by their immediate, real-world pain point.
- **Eyebrow:** DIAGNOSTICS
- **Headline:** What's broken today?
- **Subheadline:** Skip the guesswork. Select a category to find the exact diagnostic tool for the job.
- **Card Count / Elements:** 5 Tactile Buttons/Switches.
  1. Water & Leaks
  2. Pumps & Power
  3. Freeze Prep
  4. Fence Faults
  5. Remote Checks
- **CTA:** (The buttons themselves act as the CTA, toggling the relevant calculator or jumping to the specific tool page).
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** On desktop, use a left-aligned vertical list of heavy-bordered toggle buttons next to a preview pane. On mobile, stack them as large, tap-friendly full-width blocks (min 44px height).

---

## 4. Calculators Section (The Math)
**Purpose:** Provide free, immediate value by helping the operator quantify their operational losses and risks.
- **Eyebrow:** FIELD CALCULATORS
- **Headline:** Estimate the damage. Plan the fix.
- **Subheadline:** Use our field calculators to see exactly how much a problem is costing you and what it takes to solve it.
- **Card Count:** 5 Core Tool Cards.
- **Recommended CTAs per Card:**
  - *Water:* Calculate Water Loss →
  - *Pumps:* Log Pump Trouble →
  - *Freeze:* Plan Freeze Defense →
  - *Fence:* Track Fence Faults →
  - *Remote:* Verify Inspections →
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** 3-column grid on desktop. 1-column vertical scroll on mobile. Keep card descriptions short (under 18 words) so the CTA is always visible without scrolling inside the card.

---

## 5. Kits Section (The Hardware)
**Purpose:** Present the physical solutions (hardware monitors) to the quantified problems.
- **Eyebrow:** HARDWARE SOLUTIONS
- **Headline:** Rugged, Snap-On Monitors.
- **Subheadline:** You found the leak—now fix it for good with field-tested hardware that works offline and requires no engineering degree.
- **Card Count:** 3 Kit Cards (FlowWatch, PulseGuard, GateLog).
- **Recommended CTA:** Get the Right Monitor →
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** Large imagery/icons. Show clear specs (e.g., "Cellular / LoRaWAN", "10-Year Battery") in bold, mono-spaced tags.

---

## 6. Why Solve Exists (Trust & Proof)
**Purpose:** Establish blue-collar credibility, differentiate from shiny "startup" ag-tech, and build trust.
- **Headline:** Built for folks who fix things themselves.
- **Body Copy:** Farms and ranches bleed money through silent leaks, overworked pumps, and unseen fence faults. Most "smart farm" tech requires a degree in IT just to turn it on. We built Solve because rural operators don't need digital transformation—they need a simple, reliable way to find the leak, track the problem, and prove the work got done. No tech guys required.
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** High contrast section (e.g., Stone 100 text on Stone 900 background) to break up the visual flow and emphasize the manifesto.

---

- **Heading:** ⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTICE
- **Body Copy:** *Disclaimer: These figures are estimates based on standard assumptions and user inputs. This tool is for informational purposes only and does not replace professional financial, legal, engineering, or agricultural advice. We disclaim all liability for decisions, costs, losses, or damages arising from reliance on these results. Please consult qualified local professionals or certified advisors for guidance specific to your situation.*
- **Desktop/Mobile Note:** Place inside a muted, yellow-tinted box with a heavy border to resemble a hazard or warning label on machinery.
